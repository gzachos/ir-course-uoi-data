Opisthocomidae is a group of birds, the only named family within the order Opisthocomiformes. The only living representative is the hoatzin (Opisthocomus hoazin) which lives in the Amazon and the Orinoco delta in South America. Several fossil species have been identified, including one from Africa[1] and one from Europe.[2]

Description[edit]

The only living representative is the hoatzin (Opisthocomus hoazin), which lives in the Amazon and the Orinoco delta in South America.[2] Several fossil species have been identified, including one from Africa[1] and one from Europe.[2]

The hoatzin is a stunning, colourful bird from South America.[5] The hoatzin is found in the rainforests of the Amazon.[6] It has a long, permanently erected crest which gives the species a stunning silhouette. This gregarious species is often found in groups of more than 40 birds, and up to 100 birds during breeding season which occurs during rainfalls.[5] The nest is a flat platform in bush or tree above water. The chicks are semi-precocial and leave the nest at about two to three weeks of age.[5] The adults feed them with semi-digested slimy mass from their crops, and the young are fed during the first four or five months of their life.[5] The young nestlings can jump out into the water, and swim with wings and feet, in order to escape predators at nest.[5] Hoatzin embryos are known to develop very quickly compared to other birds.

The hoatzin lives in tropical forested wetlands of 200 to 500 meters elevation.[1] The hoatzin is an arboreal species and is folivorous, feeding on leaves, flowers and fruits of a small number of plant species.[2][6] It has a large crop that uses bacteria to break down the leaves that it eats.[6] The hoatzin is unique because it has bacteria in its crop that breaks down its food while it is still in the crop.[7][8][9] This is a process called foregut fermentation.[6][10] It rarely drinks because its diet includes water.[6] The hoatzin is sedentary and widespread in suitable habitat. They are noisy birds, calling in unison, uttering large numbers of varied sounds.[5]

In addition to foregut fermentation, the hoatzin has a highly modified skeleton to accommodate its large crop, and in the young of this species, wing claws at the wrist joint which are used to climb among the branches of the nest tree.[3]
