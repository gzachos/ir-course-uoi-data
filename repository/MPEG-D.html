MPEG-D

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

MPEG-D is a group of standards for audio coding formally known as ISO/IEC 23003 - MPEG audio technologies, published since 2007.[1][2][3][4]

MPEG-D consists of four parts:

  • MPEG-D Part 1: MPEG Surround[5] (a.k.a. Spatial Audio Coding[6])
  • MPEG-D Part 2: Spatial Audio Object Coding (SAOC)[7]
  • MPEG-D Part 3: Unified speech and audio coding[8]
  • MPEG-D Part 4: Dynamic Range Control[9]
